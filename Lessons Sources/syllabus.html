Course Details

Description

This course was first held in Politecnico di Milano during April and July 2014, and it’s main goal was to give to our colleagues a practical approach to engineering. We’ve prepared some papers and models to give to the students the right tools (both theoretical tools, and practical tools) to understand what is model based design, what is control theory and how they can use Simulink for building a model of what happens in the real world, simulating it, and then, for building a Real Time Control System with an Arduino board. We will analyze the dynamics and the kinematics of a BLIMP, in order to obtain it’s equation of motion, so we can modelize the system in Simulink for designing a PID controller for altitude and another PID control for yaw. Then the model is transferred to an Arduino board via Simulink Support Package for Arduino, and a Custom Simulink Toolbox developed by ours specifically for this course, in order to drive the sensors used on the Arduino Shield.


Figure 1: The BLIMP builded for the course.

In the first three lessons the students will gain the necessary theoretical basis and understandings of the BLIMP; in the fouth and the fifth lessons the studens will be guided in the practical use of Simulink. The lessons fourth and fifth are also correlated by two examples of a PID control, and some simple example that the student can use to become familiar with the Arduino\Simulink integration. The CAD of the BLIMP and the Eagle files of the Arduino shield are also packaged in the zip package with the material of the course.
